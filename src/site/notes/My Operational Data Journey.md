## Executive Profile

<div class="domain-expert-banner">
<div class="deb-icon">🧠</div>
<div class="deb-body">
<div class="deb-title">Seasoned Technology Integrator  Data Intelligence Since 2007, Hybrid Systems by Design</div>
<div class="deb-text">
Data intelligence and automation have never been a new idea here  they have been the <em>first principle</em> since day one.

The career began in <strong>2007</strong> with a focus that set the tone for everything that followed: <em>building Plant Information Management Systems (PIMS)</em> from the ground up. That meant integrating multi-vendor control systems  PLCs, DCS, SCADA  through <strong>OPC DA/A&E, Modbus RTUs, and serial field buses</strong> into centralized historian platforms, real-time data acquisition layers, and enterprise reporting systems. Delivery ownership was absolute  from <strong>alpha and beta site deployments</strong>, iterating with early customers at their facilities, through to full <strong>commercial rollout</strong> across the region. That cycle of field-validated, customer-proven delivery helped establish coverage across <strong>~75% of regional oil &amp; gas operators</strong>  not through marketing, but through systems that worked and customers who trusted them.

Over time, those PIMS foundations evolved into deeper connectivity  architecting <strong>on-premises distributed systems</strong> with redundant data pipelines, DMZ-secured historian tiers, MES integrations, and standardized alarm architectures across multi-site industrial operations.

As the industry moved upward, so did the work  into a purpose-built <strong>Managed Cloud Services Platform (CSP)</strong>, architected to manage the complete lifecycle of connected edge devices at enterprise scale. That platform extended all the way down to <strong>embedded systems</strong>  designing device-side firmware agents, secure OTA firmware delivery, provisioning workflows, and telemetry ingestion pipelines that transformed physical hardware into continuously managed, cloud-visible edge nodes. The platform scaled to <strong>50,000+ connected edge nodes</strong> at 99.7% uptime, with device identity management, remote configuration, health telemetry, and fault recovery all operating at fleet level. <strong>Edge node integration with the application development layer</strong> was a first-class architecture concern: edge nodes ran local compute and buffered data autonomously during connectivity gaps, while the cloud orchestrated fleet-wide updates, aggregated operational telemetry, and served device context as live data to engineering applications  enabling both <strong>low-latency edge autonomy</strong> and <strong>end-to-end device-to-cloud connectivity and management</strong> at global scale. Then into <strong>IoT and edge intelligence</strong> with OPC UA, MQTT, and AWS IoT Greengrass, closing the real-time feedback loop between the physical plant and cloud intelligence.

In the most recent chapter, the stack expanded further into advanced integration  including <strong>Field Device Management (FDM) diagnostics</strong> and <strong>NAMUR standards</strong> (NE 107, NE 105) to surface device health and predictive signals from the instrumentation layer itself. Today that full depth converges in <strong>AI, Digital Twins, and intelligent automation</strong>  machine learning at the edge, Unified Alarm &amp; Event Management at Level 4, performance loop optimization through continuous AI inference, and RPA workflows that make the entire operational data chain self-executing.

The result is a <strong>hybrid architecture philosophy</strong>: on-premises resilience where latency and security demand it, cloud scale where breadth requires it, AI intelligence threaded across every layer  from sensor to boardroom.

What makes this journey distinct is not just technical depth  it is <strong>ownership of delivery end-to-end</strong>, built through years of direct customer engagement, cross-functional team integration, and a natural ability to operate across every level: from a field engineer in a control room to an executive advisor in a boardroom. A <strong>strong collaborator and communicator</strong> who scales easily across people, teams, and disciplines  because the language of operational data connects all of them.
